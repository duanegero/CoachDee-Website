ABOUT

I'm Duane Gero, one of the Technical Directors of Basketball at the Community Y. With a passion for the game and a dedication to fostering young talent, I've been coaching the U12 boys' team, the Panthers, for the past decade. Under my guidance, the Panthers have secured numerous city and provincial championships, a testament to our hard work and team spirit.

My journey in basketball began at the age of 8 when I started playing for the Panthers. Growing up, I had the privilege of playing for some of the most respected coaches in the city. In high school, I played for three local schools: Halifax Grammar School, St. Pat's High, and Citadel High School. I further honed my skills during a brief stint at Concordia University. These experiences have shaped my approach to coaching, emphasizing both skill development and the importance of teamwork.

At the Community Y, I train boys and girls aged 8-16, focusing on essential basketball skills. My coaching covers offensive skills, defensive principles, how to play the game, footwork, and other basics. In addition to on-court coaching, I also mentor young coaches, sharing my knowledge and passion for the game to help them grow and succeed.

I believe in the power of basketball to build character, teach life skills, and create lasting friendships. Whether you're a player looking to improve your game or a coach seeking guidance, I'm here to support your journey in basketball. Let's work together to achieve greatness on and off the court!

Feel free to reach out to me for any coaching needs or basketball inquiries. Go Panthers!
